How It Works

Project Mandolin is an initiative to disrupt the existing structure of government and international politics. The concentration of power with policy makers and their lack of agency is a seriously flawed structure

Internatinal diplomacy has traditionally relied on the theory of representation. A wide range of beliefs and views are coalesced into a single policy. The issue is that this is an inherently flawed optimisation problem. A policy maker attempts to craft a single policy based on a variety of constraints to best meet the desires of the general population

The internet has proven to be a disruptive force, decentralising power in a variety of industries. Its distributed information architecture presents a powerful platform to connect and coalesce

The issue with representation in policy making is that it is an inherently flawed optimisation problem. A policy maker attempts to craft a single policy based on a variety of constraints to best meet the desires of the general population

In the context of war crime intervention, the UN has more recently been hampered by its need to appear neutral and its dependency on member states to contribute armed forces. A notable example of where these constraints foiled the UN's efforts is Rwanda where the lack of agency, politics and bureaucracy prevented a timely intervention

We believe the internet is a powerful tool through which private citizens can crowdfund an intervention force to stop war crimes. This force would be short term and have highly targeted objectives so as to allow time for the UN to gain the necessary approval for its own intervention force

Targeted Interventions

Project Mandolin is a controlled platform that enables private citizens to crowdfund a private security force to intervene when genocide and other crimes are occurring. Missions are designed to be short term (1-2 months) and highly targeted such that the force on the ground can be held accountable for achieving specific objectives. The objective of crowd-funded force is to deter and protect civilians and vulnerable groups from persecution and they act as a temporary force until the UN can send its own troops

This platform will shift the conversation on international policy from the collective to the individual. By leveraging the power of the internet to connect and gather, we believe Project Mandolin is a powerful platform to supplement the UN's efforts to tackle war crimes and humanitarian crises

Why are we doing this?
